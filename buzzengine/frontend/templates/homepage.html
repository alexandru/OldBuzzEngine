Open-source commenting system

TheBuzzEngine is open-source software that brings visitors comments to your webpage.

Why?

Content websites do not necessarily need complicated software for publishing an article. You just need a cheap (or even free) web-hosting solution for your static HTML content. GitHub Pages are all the rage amongst geeks these days.

Except that you need user-interaction with your content, especially comments.

How much does it cost?

The software itself is free of charge and open-source. Hosting is provided by Google's App Engine and is also free of charge for low-traffic websites (up to a quota). In practice the popularity of your website will have to be huge before shelling out a couple of bucks per month.

What about DISQUS or Facebook?

  • content isn't yours, and in the case of Facebook moderation of comments gets tricky
  • these are companies providing a service which may be discontinued at some point. User comments are a valuable part of your website that may vanish
  • Facebook isn't known for good ethics and DISQUS is too young. If you value your users, placing bets on companies who do not generate income directly from you is tricky and your faith must be strong ;)
  • basically TheBuzzEngine is all about control and simplicity

Technicalities

The application is built to run on top of Google App Engine. Scalability is theoretically unlimited and the usage simple.

After setting up a Google App Engine account and deploying on it, all you need to do is copy/paste a Javascript widget in your web pages were you want comments.
